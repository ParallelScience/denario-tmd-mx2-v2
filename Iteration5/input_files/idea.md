**Title: Electronic Phase-Space Mapping of Mechanical Robustness in Metastable TMDs**

This study develops a binary classification framework to identify mechanically robust metastable TMDs by defining a stability boundary in the feature space of `d_band_filling`, `en_difference`, and `dos_at_fermi`. Rather than regressing continuous elastic constants, the model uses the 90-sample elastic dataset to characterize the electronic signatures of mechanical softening, specifically targeting materials with $G_{vrh}$ exceeding the median threshold of the stable population. By incorporating `magnetic_ordering` and `M_soc_proxy` as critical features, the model accounts for spin-dependent structural distortions—such as Jahn-Teller effects—that suppress mechanical stability in high-density-of-states phases. The classification objective is to screen the 112 metastable candidates for "mechanical viability," using a random forest ensemble to handle the non-linear interaction between crystal symmetry and electronic filling. The final output is a probability map that filters metastable TMDs, isolating those with high predicted shear moduli to prioritize candidates for experimental synthesis.
