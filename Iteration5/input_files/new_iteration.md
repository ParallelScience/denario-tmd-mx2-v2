- Decision: ITERATE
- Justification: The current project has successfully established a foundational link between electronic structure and mechanical properties, but the identified limitations—specifically the negative $R^2$ in cross-validation and the reliance on PBE-level artifacts for late transition metals—place the current results in a "Borderline" state. The proposed changes are not merely incremental; they represent a fundamental shift from "interpolating PBE data" to "predicting physical viability." Implementing a group-aware model, shifting the target to the physically robust Pugh’s ratio ($G/K$), and incorporating a high-level DFT validation step (HSE06/PBE+U) directly addresses the "Reviewer Confidence" and "Structural Robustness" criteria. These improvements will transform the manuscript from a descriptive study of existing data into a predictive discovery framework, significantly increasing the "Acceptance Delta" for a top-tier materials science journal. The ROI is high because the infrastructure for the multi-task model is already in place, and the proposed refinements leverage existing data more effectively rather than requiring a complete restart.
