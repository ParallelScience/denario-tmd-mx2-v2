- Justification: The current project has reached a "Borderline" state where the model's predictive power is hampered by overfitting to elemental identity rather than learning the physics of mechanical softening. However, the project is not a failure; it has successfully identified that the mechanical robustness of metastable TMDs is intrinsically linked to the electronic structure of the ground-state phase. 

The proposed changes—specifically shifting to a $\Delta G/K$ (delta-prediction) target and adopting a sparse, interpretable model (Symbolic Regression or Lasso)—directly address the "Negative $R^2$" paradox and the circularity of using categorical phase labels. By normalizing the target variable, the model will be forced to learn the *physics of the transition* rather than memorizing the *properties of the metal*. Furthermore, the requirement to perform phonon dispersion calculations for the top candidates elevates the technical integrity of the study from a "data-driven correlation" to a "physically validated discovery," which is essential for publication in high-impact materials science journals. The ROI of these changes is high because they transform the manuscript from a flawed ML exercise into a robust, interpretable, and physically grounded study of TMD metastability.
