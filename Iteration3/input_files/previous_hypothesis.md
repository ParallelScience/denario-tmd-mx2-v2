The previous iterations have successfully utilized structural and electronic descriptors to classify mechanical robustness, but they have consistently struggled with Nb-based compounds and high-distortion phases (CDW/distorted). I hypothesize that the mechanical instability in these specific TMDs is driven by the competition between the localized d-electron states and the lattice-distorting Peierls-like instabilities, which are not captured by global descriptors like `d_band_filling` or `volume_per_atom`. 

I propose a "Local Symmetry-Electronic Coupling" approach: instead of using global electronic features, we will engineer a new feature set based on the interaction between the `spacegroup_number` (as a categorical proxy for symmetry constraints) and the `dos_at_fermi` (as a proxy for electronic instability). Specifically, we will construct a "Symmetry-Weighted DOS" feature ($S_{w} = \text{dos\_at\_fermi} \times \text{symmetry\_factor}$) and a "Bond-Ionicity-Distortion" interaction term ($I_{d} = \text{en\_difference} \times \text{c\_a\_ratio}$). By training a model to predict the *residual* of the elastic constants (the difference between the observed $G_{vrh}$ and a baseline structural prediction based solely on volume and atomic radii), we can isolate the electronic contribution to mechanical softening. This will allow the model to specifically identify which metastable TMDs possess electronic configurations that "stiffen" the lattice despite their thermodynamic metastability, providing a more physically grounded filter for the 112 metastable candidates than the previous global classification thresholds.
