The persistent underperformance of mechanical robustness models on Nb-based TMDs suggests that the current feature set fails to capture the specific electronic-structural coupling inherent to Charge Density Wave (CDW) transitions. I hypothesize that the mechanical instability in these materials is driven by the competition between the Fermi surface nesting (FSN) and the local d-orbital hybridization, which is not fully represented by `dos_at_fermi` or `d_band_filling`. 

To address this, I propose constructing a "CDW-Susceptibility Index" (CSI) feature. This index will be derived from the interaction between `dos_at_fermi` and the `c_a_ratio` (as a proxy for structural anisotropy) normalized by the `M_soc_proxy`. By training a model to predict the residual error of the previous mechanical robustness classifier specifically for Nb and Ta-based compounds, we can isolate the "CDW-penalty" on the shear modulus. This approach shifts the focus from global mechanical prediction to identifying the specific electronic signatures that trigger structural distortions, allowing for a more accurate filtering of metastable candidates that are prone to CDW-induced softening rather than intrinsic mechanical fragility.
